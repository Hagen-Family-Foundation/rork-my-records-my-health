Onboarding flow

What to enter first so the record becomes useful quickly

The goal is not to fill everything in perfectly on day one. The goal is to capture the most important information first, then strengthen the record over time.

💊

Medications

Add current medication names, dosages, schedules, and any notes that help a doctor, nurse, caregiver, or EMT understand what is being taken now.

⚠️

Allergies and reactions

List allergies clearly and include the reaction whenever possible so the most urgent safety information is hard to miss.

🩺

Diagnoses and medical history

Record conditions, surgeries, procedures, chronic issues, and other key facts that help explain the person’s broader health picture.

📞

Emergency contacts

Add the people who should be reached first, including their role, phone number, and anything helpful a responder should know.

👨‍⚕️

Physician and care team details

Enter primary care, specialists, clinic details, and other provider information that may help coordinate care faster.

🌍

Language and communication preferences

Note preferred language and communication needs so care can be clearer, gentler, and more effective in urgent situations.

Frequently asked

Questions a first-time customer may still have

Do I need to complete every field at once?

No. Start with the most important information first, then come back to strengthen the record over time.

What should I enter first if I feel overwhelmed?

Begin with medications, allergies, diagnoses, emergency contacts, physician details, and language or communication preferences.

How do the app and wallet card work together?

The app holds the organized record, while the wallet card helps make essential information easier to reach quickly in urgent situations.

Can I use this for a parent, spouse, child, or dependent?

Yes. The messaging and setup flow are built to support seniors, caregivers, people with disabilities or special needs, and families managing care for someone else.

What if I need help before I finish setup?

Use the support page or email support so you can keep moving with confidence instead of stopping halfway through the process.
